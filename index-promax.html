Act I · The Approach
Essay
“Every point is a puzzle. My job is to hand you the pieces — and the language to put them together.”
Jeff Zipman

Thirty years ago, Jeff picked up a clipboard and a camcorder and started coaching tennis in Vancouver. The equipment has changed. The method has not. Every lesson, private or group, ends the same way: a short film of your game, a conversation about what the camera saw, and two or three quiet drills to take home until next time.

The result is a record of your tennis — stroke by stroke, year after year. It’s why so many of his players stay for decades, and why a handful of them are now parents bringing their own juniors to the same courts.

Act III · The Film
Feature
The Process

See your game from the baseline up.

Every private lesson ends with a film. A quiet review, the clip paused at the right frame, and two or three drills to take home until next week. The camera has been rolling for thirty years — the method hasn’t changed.

  1. i.
    On court, we film

    A phone or tablet at the baseline captures the lesson as it runs. Nothing interrupts the hour.

  2. ii.
    Off court, we review

    Side-by-side footage, a few stills, and plain-English notes on what the camera saw that you didn’t.

  3. iii.
    Between lessons, we work

    Two or three specific drills matched to the clips — so the next session starts ahead of the last one.
